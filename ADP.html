Introducing the Accessible Diagnosis Project, an AVC initiative aiming to make diagnosis availible to all

What does the Accessible
Diagnosis Project do?

  • We fund the

    full cost

    of the diagnositic process, from initial evaluation to final result.
  • We connect you with

    reputable professionals

    to ensure an accurate and positive process.
  • We provide you with connections to

    supportive services

    and

    non-fear-based resources

    to learn more about the autistic community.

Why is this initiative important?

  • Autistic people, more often than not, face a whole host of difficulties when deciding to seek an official diagnosis; these difficulties only increase for AFAB and older individuals. Often getting a professional who is

    familiar with more than one view of autism

    is difficult; older individuals have often masked heavily their whole life, so many write them off neurotypical.
  • However, even after doing research and talking to the right people, many still are unable to get a diagnosis due to steep costs; the whole process can cost

    up to $5000.

  • Getting an official autism diagnosis allows autistic children, teens, and adult alike to access

    resources and accomodations at school and work

    that they couldn't have gotten otherwise. Furthermore, for many the diagnosis is validating on a personal level. Many late-diagnosed people have felt out of place and confused with themselves for years; diagnosis actually

    helps self-image and mental health

    in this way.

What masking as an autistic person feels like: Elsa Frankel's story with masking in highschool and middle school

June 27, 2021, 7:00pm EST

Written by: Elsa Frankel

Masking is something many autistic people do in attempts to fit in with the Neurotypical social structure. While it might look like we are feeling no different than usual from the outside, masking is very tiring to autistic people, and can cause us mental distress if we are unable to unmask at some point.

before I was recieved my official autism Diagnosis in 8th grade, I was masking constantly; I didn't know it at the time of course, but that's what I was doing. During my early middle school years I was pretty much what you would call a loner, or as I liked to call myself, "an extreme" introvert. Socializing stressed me out beyond belief, but even more than that, it exhasted me. I found myself constantly craving time away from people; when I got that time, it felt almost freeing. On top of that, I was always very hard on myself for not being "as good at socializing" as my peers were. In the harder moments I would tell myself to just stop talking to people because I "couldn't do it well enough." My masking was fairly intense, and well, clearly wasn't always working as well as I would have liked.
